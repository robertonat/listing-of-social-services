The first thing to consider when looking at this data is the percentage makeup. Data detailing where wifi hotspots are make up over half the entries.

However even taking away the data that was provided by wifi hotspots shows some discrepency between the remaining results
Child care and women oriented resources make up the majority of the remaining data sets.

This is simply due to the difference in amount of data avalible for each tag. The wifi had about 3000+ data entries while some others had less than 100. It is important to also note the difference in infrastructure between the tags. Wifi hotspots are easier to set up and scatter around than entire buildings funded by organizations full of volunteers and administrative individuals. Another interesting analysis to look at would be the make up of what is in each borough. Unlike the tags it is possible that the data entries would have split their locations evenly amoung the five boroughs.
